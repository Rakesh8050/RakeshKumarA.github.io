NGS-Based Pipeline for Rapid Pathogen Detection

Major Project

Designed and implemented an automated, reproducible, and scalable bioinformatics workflow using Snakemake for rapid pathogen detection and classification from Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) data, enabling efficient microbial profiling for clinical diagnostics and public health surveillance.

Key Objectives:

  • Automate data pre-processing including quality control, adapter trimming, and host read filtering.
  • Utilize Kraken2 for fast and accurate taxonomic classification against curated reference databases.
  • Quantify pathogen abundance using Bracken to provide clinical and epidemiological insights.
  • Generate intuitive, interactive visual reports with Krona to facilitate interpretation.

Tools & Technologies:

Snakemake FastQC TrimGalore Bowtie2 Kraken2 Bracken Krona

AI/ML Analysis of TCGA - LUSC | Lung Cancer Gene Expression Dataset

Personal Project

Applied machine learning models to gene expression data from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) to classify lung squamous cell carcinoma (LUSC) samples. The project involved a full data science pipeline from preprocessing and dimensionality reduction to model training and evaluation, aiming to identify the most effective classification algorithm for this bioinformatics task.

Key Findings:

  • Successfully preprocessed and reduced the high-dimensional dataset using PCA, retaining 64.3% of variance with 120 components.
  • The K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) model demonstrated superior performance, achieving a perfect balanced accuracy of 1.0 and precision of 0.9934.
  • Confirmed the high quality of the dataset and the effectiveness of the preprocessing pipeline, as all models performed well.

Tools & Technologies:

Python Scikit-learn Pandas PCA Logistic Regression KNN Random Forest XGBoost

In Silico Design of a Multi-Epitope Vaccine for Streptococcus

Minor Project

Leveraged a comprehensive pipeline of immunoinformatics and molecular simulation tools to design and validate a chimeric, multi-epitope vaccine against Group A Streptococcus (GAS). The vaccine targets key GAS virulence factors—M protein and FbaA—by integrating predicted B-cell and T-cell epitopes with a potent adjuvant to elicit a robust immune response.

Key Results:

  • Constructed a stable, non-allergenic, and highly antigenic 601 amino acid vaccine candidate.
  • Structural validation confirmed a high-quality 3D model with 94.16% of residues in favored regions.
  • Molecular docking showed strong, stable binding to key immune receptors (TLR4, MHC-I, MHC-II).
  • Immune simulations predicted a robust response with high levels of immunoglobulins and memory cell formation.
